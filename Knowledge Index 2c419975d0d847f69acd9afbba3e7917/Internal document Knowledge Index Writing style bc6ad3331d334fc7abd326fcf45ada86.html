This is specific to the Knowledge Index writing style, and not a general purpose writing style

Everyone has a different way to share knowledge. This writing style is specific to the Knowledge Index, is opinionated, and is no more or less valid than other approaches to knowledge sharing.

This is debateable

Participants can talk about it in the #writing-style channel on Discord.

Use pictures

Pictures are worth a thousand words.

The following two pictures are used to describe Why should I use Baked lighting if I can use Realtime lighting?

This gets the point across.

Other knowledge sharing resources outside of Knowledge Index may be more elaborate, and they should be. Silent provides a in-depth description of why you should bake lights, with factual information. This is good to provide a solid foundation, and not the approach we're taking here.

Pictures should provide a convincing kick start and should not overwhelm the reader with lots of information to process.

Raise awareness

Knowledge Index is not really a place for tutorials to start learning a new thing. You won't really see a tutorial about how to write UdonSharp here, or how to create a world.

Instead, we want to raise awareness about things that we could do better if we knew it existed before. Obscure pitfalls to avoid so that you don't rediscover what some of us have already learned the hard way.

We want to initiate the reader's own curiosity about a subject for them to learn more on their own, and maybe point them in the right direction.

Education primes over being Rigorous

Getting ideas across is more important than being absolutely accurate and pedantic about a subject.

The title is the summary

If you read all the titles of a page, you can infer the content.

Index it sooner than later

We're not trying to build a perfectly formatted documentation. Index the knowledge when we learn something, it does not require the commitment of a project and it can start small.

Don't use complex engineering terminology

Don't: Encapsulate your fields

Do: Make your field editable but not accessible by external UdonBehaviours

We're not trying to speak to engineers or train some. These are incomprehensible words that are unhelpful to most.

For example don't use words like "domain" (referring to Domain Driven Design) or anything to do with "agility". If the reader can understand these words anyways, they are probably already software developers in a professional capacity who don't need this taught to them.

If a word is used on a regular basis in discussions even by newcomers, it could be considered for usage. The word "retopology" is one of them.
