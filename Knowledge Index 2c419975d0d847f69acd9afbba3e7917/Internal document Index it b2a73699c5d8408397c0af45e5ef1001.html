When you learn something, "Index it"

A knowledge entry is like a bug report. If you don't report a bug, it won't get fixed.

Well if you don't index it, people will still fall into it.

See: Raise awareness

Notion accounts and Privacy

Participating in the public indexing is currently limited to a closed group of participants while we set things up and see if it can work out.

You must have a Notion account. When creating your account, consider the following privacy concerns:

The name, profile icon, and the email address you choose will be visible by collaborators.

Assume that a collaborator could be any random person.

  • Consider creating an anonymized email redirect if personal information like your first name or last name is visible in the email address.
  • You should probably not use the same email as the account used on social platforms like VRChat.

When registering by email:

  • The name can be edited anytime in Settings & Members > My Account > Personal Info / Preferred name.
  • Same goes for profile icon.
  • The email can be changed with a security process to be followed.

I don't know what happens if you register via Google.

Also, you may want to change Settings & Members > Workspace/Settings > Name and Domain, as they could possibly show up in some places, such as in embedding when you share your own personal pages with others.

Jot it down in Notion

You may choose to create the thread on Discord first, but you are encouraged to jot it down even if you're not sure.

We'll refer to it as an Entry in the Index; a piece of knowledge.

  • Open this page:
  • Add a new title and start writing your entry. Don't wait for permission.
  • All entries being written are visible by the public, updated in real time as you type it. There is no publish process. Make sure you don't paste or drag-and-drop sensitive information by accident. If you paste an image from your clipboard or drag-and-drop a resource, it will be instantly uploaded with no confirmation dialog, keep that in mind.
  • If your entry ends up being misleading, it's fine, it can be removed. Discord will give a second opinion through the thread. You should show your entry to members of your own community to see if they can validate it.

Open a thread in Discord

The order does not matter, you can create the thread first if you want.

You don't have to complete writing the entry to open the thread.

  • Create a thread in the #index-it🧵 channel by clicking the + sign > Create thread.
  • Title the thread like you would title that entry. See:
  • Copy the thread title and paste it in the Starter message field.
  • Confirm creation of the thread.

Lifetime of the thread and entry being written

  • Don't worry about the title of the thread, if it diverges from the actual entry it's fine.
  • Don't edit the Starter message, even if the thread or entry title changes.
  • When you think your entry is complete, add the bell 🔔 emoji at the end of the entry in Notion. This is your decision and you don't have to wait for any of the others to answer.
  • The thread is archived and discussion about that entry should be in the #general channel.

Licensing of the entries

We haven't yet determined licensing of the text.

MIT is a software license, and even though it can apply to documentation of software, we're not exactly writing software documentation here, so I still need to check this out if this can still be applied.

If there's code (i.e. C# for Unity entries, UdonSharp for VRChat entries, Python for Blender entries), we'll probably request it to be MIT.

Pictures of assets and avatars

Also yet to be determined.

When it comes to avatars, use an avatar that you have created yourself entirely from scratch, or that you have purchased on online stores like Booth.pm.

Make sure that you check the license of that avatar.

You cannot use an avatar that is strongly associated with a brand (i.e. Vket mascots), with the exception of the brand of the social platforms themselves when your entry is directly related to that social platform (i.e. gray robot for VRChat entries is fine).

Make sure that the assets that are visible on pictures have a license compatible with what is being done within the context of the entry.

If the picture comes from the Internet, check the license of the picture, like Wikimedia.
