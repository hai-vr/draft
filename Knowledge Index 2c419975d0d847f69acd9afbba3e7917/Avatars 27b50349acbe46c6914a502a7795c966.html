Animations cannot animate the blendshapes used by the Avatar Descriptor Eye Look blendshapes

If you cannot animate the eyes closing, check that you are not using that blendshape as the Blink Blendshape inside the Avatar's Eye Tracking settings.

Many avatar bases have a blendshape to close the left eye and the right eye independently. If applicable, you can try animating them both instead in your animation.

Note that disabling eye tracking will let you animate the Blink blendshape, but I discourage doing it because changing eye from Animation to Tracking is instantaneous and will not result in a smooth transition.

Visemes will not function if any animation animates them

Viseme animations when speaking will not work if there is any animation that animates the visemes.

Viseme animation blendshapes should not be used for face expression animations.

Proxy animations cannot be modified

If you cannot animate a locomotion pose animation (i.e. Idle animation) or an action post animation (i.e. AFK animation), check that you are not modifying a proxy animation. Proxy animations usually have a name that start with proxy_.

If you are, duplicate that animation, and re-assign it within your animator.

The only purpose of proxy animations is to tell VRChat to replace it with a real animation when the avatar loads.

Use a Position Constraint to fix Cloth components spazzing out of control

The avatar's root position will sometime drift away from the actual center of the player as time goes by. This can cause an issue where slightly turning your head would cause the cloth to glitch out of control, as if it was spinning fast.

Add a Position Constraint on your Cloth, and constraint it to the Hip bone of your avatar. This should alleviate the issue.

Make sure all SkinnedMeshRenderers have the same Bounds and an Anchor Override so that the avatar is shaded uniformly

If your avatar has multiple SkinnedMeshRenderers, you need to make sure the Bounds of all SkinnedMeshRenderers are the same.

In addition, assign an Anchor Override to all meshes of your avatar. You can set the Anchor Override to be a GameObject parented to the Chest or the Hip of your avatar.

The Bounds serve two purposes:

  • The light probe will be correctly sampled from the same location for all skinned meshes. That way, the shading style of the head will not look different from the shading style of the body.
  • When the avatar is at the edge of the vision of the VR headset from the point of view of other players, the mesh will not suddenly disappear while still in view, nor disappear at a different moment than other parts of the mesh.

If a SkinnedMeshRenderer is a Cloth component, you cannot edit its Bounds. Leave them as is.

The Anchor Override makes sure that the Reflection Probe that is being sampled is the same for the entire avatar, which can affect the shading style.

Testing that change will not be accurate in mirrors when looking at yourself

When testing, you need to ignore the mirror because the avatar will look different when you look at yourself in the mirror locally, than when other people will look at you.

Use EditorOnly to exclude things on your avatar

Objects that are tagged as EditorOnly will not be included in your avatar when it builds.

This includes the avatar performance ranking.

The performance ranking in the build window may look different from when the avatar is inspected in-game.

Disabling mouth tracking stops updating the Viseme parameter

If you disable mouth tracking, the Viseme parameter will no longer update.

Disabling finger tracking does not stop updating the Gesture parameters

If you disable finger tracking, the Gesture parameters will continue to update.

This is useful to continue detecting hand gestures while still playing hand animations.

Cycle Offset does nothing

After trying to tinker with Cycle Offset, we have concluded that it effectively does nothing, at least in Unity 2019. This contradicts the documentation about it.

If you are using Cycle Offset to actually do something that works, please contact us, we want to know.

When using avatar masks, any animation that swaps materials of a renderer needs to have its transform allowed in the mash

If you use avatar masks, and you have animations that swaps the material of any renderer (Mesh, Skinned Mesh, Trail, etc.), you need to allow the transform of the renderer in your avatar mask.

You don't need to allow any of its parents in the hierarchy.
